Pakistan E-Commerce Ecosystem Analysis (2016-2018) 🇵🇰📊
Executive Summary
This project performs a deep-dive Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a dataset of over 500,000 transactions from the Pakistan E-commerce ecosystem. The analysis reveals a market dominated by mobile technology but hampered by payment infrastructure challenges and seasonal fluctuations.

🛠️ Tech Stack & Libraries
Language: Python

Data Manipulation: Pandas

Visualization: Matplotlib

Dataset Shape: 1,048,575 rows and 26 columns

🎯 Key Analysis Questions
To drive actionable business recommendations, the project focuses on:

Product Performance: Identifying top sales and volume categories.

Payment Reliability: Comparing successful completions vs. high cancellation rates across payment methods.

Temporal Trends: Impact of seasonality (Months/Days) on order density.

High-Value Audit: Analyzing the completion rates of premium/expensive items.

Counter-Intuitive Patterns: Identifying hidden behaviors like the "Premium Transaction Paradox".

📈 Insights & Findings
1. Category Performance
Top Drivers: Mobiles & Tablets lead with over 115,000 orders, followed by Men's Fashion.

Market Insight: The platform is a primary revenue driver for electronics, but there is a growing high-variety segment in Women's fashion.

2. Payment Method & Reliability Audit
COD Dominance: Cash on Delivery remains the most trusted method by Pakistani consumers.

Infrastructure Bottleneck: Digital payments (EasyPay/JazzCash) suffer from high cancellation rates due to technical failures during checkout.

Bank Transfers: High drop-off rate observed for premium items.

3. The "November Effect" (Seasonality)
Order Volume: Orders in November exceed the average monthly volume by nearly 300%.

Key Events: Driven by major sales events like 11.11 and Blessed Friday.

Peak Volume: Reaches over 150,000+ orders in a single month.

4. The Premium Transaction Paradox
Analysis of high-value transactions (>300,000 PKR) reveals a 0% completion rate in the top 10 segment.

These items are consistently canceled or refunded, indicating a systemic failure in fulfilling luxury or high-end electronics orders.

💡 Recommendations
Scale Infrastructure: Increase server capacity and logistics support specifically for the Q4 period (November).

Checkout Optimization: Implement mandatory call-verification for orders exceeding 50,000 PKR to reduce high-value cancellations.
